About Me

My name is Harin Lyu, and I was born in South Korea. Although I spent my early childhood there, my education has been entirely international. I moved to Singapore in first grade and began my schooling at United World College (UWCSEA). I am currently in eighth grade at Tanglin Trust School (TTS), where I continue to pursue both academic and extracurricular passions. Academically, I am fascinated in all parts of Science, whether its Biology, Chemistry, or Physics I tend to be drawn to all. I also particularly enjoy History and Art. These subjects allow me to think critically, explore creativity, and make connections between the past and present. Beyond the classroom, I am a student athlete and a performer. I play competitive volleyball representing my school, play Squash at the American Club of Singapore, and enjoy running in my free time, and boxing to improve my strength and agility. I also sing in both the senior and chamber choirs, and sometimes engage in community service activities that allow me to give back to my community. These experiences have helped shape me into a well rounded student with a global perspective and a strong sense of purpose.

UNESCO Art Competition Nominee

Certificate of Honor presented in Troyes, France, April 2022

My artistic creation was honored with a Certificate of Honor from L'Institut Mondial d'Art de la Jeunesse - Observatoire de l'UNESCO. This recognition signifies that my artwork, themed "Le paysage que j'aime" (The landscape I love), was presented at the international "Graines d'artistes du monde entier" competition and has been registered in the art collection heritage, serving as a testament to global youth's cultural richness and creativity.
